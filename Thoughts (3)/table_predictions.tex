\subsection{Comparison of Detection Prospects Across GW Observatories}

The stochastic gravitational-wave background (SGWB) from twist-defect mergers peaks sharply at $f_{\rm peak} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{-28}$ Hz ($\sim 7.3$ nHz), with exponentially suppressed amplitude outside this ultra-low-frequency band. The table below compares the predicted signal strength against the sensitivity of current and planned detectors. All values are derived from the finite prefix $T=10^{10}$ (no free parameters).

\textbf{Key notes}:
\begin{itemize}
    \item The peak signal lies at nHz frequencies, accessible only to advanced PTAs (SKA-PTA, NANOGrav 20+ yr, IPTA DR3+).
    \item All higher-frequency detectors (LISA, DECIGO, BBO) lie in the exponential tail of the spectrum, where $\Omega_{\rm GW} \approx 0$.
    \item A positive detection at any frequency above $\sim 10^{-15}$ Hz would contradict the finite-prefix cutoff and falsify the framework.
\end{itemize}
